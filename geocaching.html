In Plain Sight

A publication that documents a selection of geocaches hidden around Melbourne. For the uninitiated, geocaching is basically treasure-hunting in real life. Except instead of gold, all you get are dollar-store trinkets and the right to scribble your name on a scrap of paper.

Exploring parts of your city you might otherwise overlook is a wonderful aspect of geocaching. The textures I encountered were almost as important as the caches themselves.
A4, coptic stitch, laser etched cover, original photography
